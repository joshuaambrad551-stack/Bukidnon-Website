About the Park

Dahilayan Adventure Park

Nestled in the cool highlands of Manolo Fortich, Bukidnon at 1,300 meters above sea level, Dahilayan Adventure Park is the Philippines' premier highland adventure destination. It is famous for housing Asia's longest dual zipline — an 840-meter flight over lush Bukidnon forests.

The park's cool climate — averaging 18–24°C year-round — makes it a refreshing escape from the lowland heat. Whether you're an adrenaline junkie or a family looking for outdoor fun, Dahilayan has something for everyone.

Activities & Attractions

From sky-high ziplines to ATV rides and freefall drops — pure adrenaline awaits.

🪂

Dual Zipline

Asia's longest dual zipline stretches 840 meters across Dahilayan's lush valley. Race side-by-side with a companion at breathtaking speeds for a bird's-eye view of Bukidnon's highland forests.

⭐ Signature Ride₱400 – ₱600
🏍️

ATV Ride

Hop on an All-Terrain Vehicle and conquer rugged forested trails. Routes wind through pine trees and highland terrain — thrilling off-road adventure for teens and adults.

Off-Road Thrills₱300 – ₱600

Dropzone

A free-fall platform that sends you plummeting toward the valley before a harness snaps you back. Perfect for thrill-seekers who want the ultimate rush at Dahilayan.

Extreme Thrill₱200 – ₱350

Gravity Rides

Enjoy the Sky Bike, Human Gyroscope, and Giant Swing — each offering a unique way to experience height and speed surrounded by misty mountain views.

Explore lush pine forest trails at your own pace. Cool highland air, towering trees, and occasional wildlife sightings offer a peaceful contrast to the park's thrill rides.

Before You Go

Visitor Tips

Make the most of your Dahilayan visit with these practical tips.

🧥

Dress in Layers

Dahilayan averages 18–24°C. Bring a jacket especially for early mornings and evenings on the mountain.

⏰

Arrive Early

Opens at 7:00–8:00 AM. Early arrival means shorter queues for the zipline and ATV rides on weekends and holidays.

👟

Wear Proper Shoes

Closed-toe shoes are required for all rides and trail walks. Flip-flops and sandals are not allowed.

🚌

How to Get There

From Cagayan de Oro City, take a bus or van to Manolo Fortich (1.5–2 hrs), then a habal-habal to the park entrance.

💳

Bring Cash

Most rides need separate ticket purchases. Budget ₱1,500–₱3,000 per person. ATMs are limited in the area.

📵

Limited Signal

Mobile signal is weak inside. Download maps offline before heading up and share your location with someone.
