Festivals & Culture

Celebrating Indigenous Heritage

2025

KAAMULAN FESTIVAL

March 21 – April 23

The Kaamulan Festival is the Philippines' only authentic tribal festival, celebrating the rich traditions of Bukidnon's seven indigenous groups. Experience colorful rituals, tribal dances, and a vibrant showcase of indigenous heritage.

Celebrity Night — April 13: TJ Monterde & KZ Tandingan
#Kaamulan2025 #Bukidnon #CulturalFestival
Higaonon

Mountain dwellers known for beadwork and oral literature

Manobo

One of the oldest indigenous groups in Mindanao

Talaandig

Artists and musicians known for gong-playing traditions

Matigsalug

Known for their elaborate rituals and weaving crafts

Tigwahanon

River people with deep connections to nature spirits

Umayamnon

Highland farmers preserving ancient agricultural rites

Food & Delicacies

Taste of Bukidnon

From highland vegetables to sweet native treats, Bukidnon offers a flavorful culinary journey.

Sinuglaw

A classic Mindanao dish combining grilled pork and fresh kinilaw — smoky, tangy, and uniquely Mindanaoan.

Bukidnon Pineapple

World-renowned Del Monte pineapples grown in the province's vast plantations — sweet, juicy, and premium quality.

Native Kakanin

Traditional rice cakes and native sweets passed down through indigenous communities, made with local ingredients.

Grilled Corn

Highland corn grilled over charcoal — a simple roadside treat that captures the agricultural spirit of Bukidnon.

Travel Tips

Plan Your Visit

Getting There

  • Fly to Cagayan de Oro (CDO) or Valencia City
  • Bus routes from CDO to Malaybalay (~2 hrs)
  • Van rentals available at CDO terminal

Best Time to Visit

  • March–May: Kaamulan Festival season
  • November–February: Coolest highland weather
  • Avoid heavy rains: July–September

Accommodations

  • Budget inns in Malaybalay City
  • Eco-lodges near Dahilayan
  • Farm stays in Manolo Fortich

Safety Tips

  • Bring a jacket — highlands get cold at night
  • Hire registered local guides for treks
  • Respect indigenous sacred sites
