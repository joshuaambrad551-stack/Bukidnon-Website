About the Ranch

Communal Ranch Overview

The Communal Ranch is a vast open space managed by the Provincial Government of Bukidnon, designed to showcase sustainable farming and ranching practices. Visitors can explore lush pastures, interact with farm animals, and learn about communal livestock management in a serene highland setting.

The ranch promotes agricultural education while offering a peaceful retreat for families and tourists. Facilities include animal pens, grazing areas, walking trails, and an observation deck for viewing ranch activities. Seasonal workshops and demonstrations provide hands-on experiences in ranch management, farming techniques, and eco-friendly practices.

Whether you are a student, a tourist, or a local resident, the Communal Ranch offers a unique opportunity to connect with nature, learn about sustainable agriculture, and enjoy outdoor recreation in a scenic environment.

Horseback Riding Experience

One of Bukidnon's most memorable and scenic equestrian experiences.

Ride Through Green Pastures

The Communal Ranch's horseback riding experience lets visitors glide across wide-open highland meadows on well-trained horses. The backdrop of rolling green hills and the cool Bukidnon breeze make this an unforgettable activity for the whole family.

Trained guides accompany every rider to ensure safety and comfort. Routes are designed for all skill levels — from first-timers to experienced equestrians — with longer trail options available for those who want a deeper adventure into the ranch grounds.

Planning Your Visit

Best Time to Visit

Timing your visit right ensures the most enjoyable ranch experience.

☀️

Dry Season

November to April is the best time to visit. Sunny skies, dry pastures, and lush green scenery make outdoor activities like horseback riding and farm walks most enjoyable.

🌅

Morning Hours

Visit between 6:00 AM – 9:00 AM for the most stunning scenery. Morning mist rolls over the highland pastures, the air is crisp and cool, and animals are most active.

🎪

Festival Season

Plan your visit during the Kaamulan Festival (March–April) to combine your ranch experience with Bukidnon's biggest cultural celebration and indigenous performances.

📅

Weekdays Preferred

Visit on weekdays (Tuesday–Thursday) for smaller crowds, more personal attention from guides, and easier access to all ranch facilities and activities.

Before You Go

Travel Guide & Visitor Tips

Everything you need for a comfortable and enjoyable ranch experience.

🚌

How to Get There

The ranch is located in Malaybalay City, Bukidnon. From the city center, take a jeepney or tricycle to the ranch. Taxis and ride-shares are also available. From CDO, the ride is about 2–3 hours.

👟

Wear Comfortable Clothes

Wear light, breathable clothing and closed-toe shoes. The ranch terrain involves walking on grass, mud, and uneven paths — sandals and heels are not suitable.

📷

Bring a Camera

The ranch's wide open meadows, roaming livestock, and highland backdrop make for stunning photography. Golden hour (5–6 PM) lighting is especially beautiful over the pastures.

👨‍👩‍👧

Great for Groups

The ranch is ideal for school trips, family outings, and team-building events. Group packages and guided tours can be arranged in advance by contacting the ranch directly.

💰

Budget & Fees

Entrance fees are generally affordable. Budget around ₱200–₱600 per person depending on activities chosen. Horseback riding, workshops, and guided tours cost extra.

🐄

Respect the Animals

Always follow the guide's instructions when near livestock. Avoid loud noises or sudden movements that could startle the animals. Do not feed animals without permission.
