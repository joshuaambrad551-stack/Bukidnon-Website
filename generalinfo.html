Traveler’s Guide

Planning your trip to the highland paradise is easy. Here is everything you need to know about transportation, climate, and local etiquette.

The primary entry point is through **Laguindingan Airport (CGY)**. From the airport, take a shuttle or taxi to the Agora Bus Terminal in Cagayan de Oro. Buses and vans depart frequently for Malaybalay City (approx. 2-3 hours) and Valencia City (approx. 3-4 hours).

Bukidnon is known as the "Summer Capital of Mindanao" due to its cool weather. Daytime temperatures range from 20°C to 28°C, but can drop to 15°C at night in higher altitudes. **Recommendation:** Always bring a light jacket and comfortable walking shoes.

For outdoor activities, the dry season from **March to May** is best. However, if you want to experience the soul of the province, visit during the **Kaamulan Festival** in August or September for the most authentic cultural experience in the Philippines.

Currency

Philippine Peso (PHP). Major cities like Valencia and Malaybalay have plenty of ATMs, but bring cash for rural adventures.

Language

Bisaya/Cebuano is the primary language. English and Tagalog are widely understood and used in government and business.

Safe Travels

Bukidnon is one of the most peaceful provinces in Mindanao. Locals are known for their warm hospitality and respect for nature.
