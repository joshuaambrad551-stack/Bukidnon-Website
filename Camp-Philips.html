About the Destination

Camp Philips Overview

Camp Philips in Manolo Fortich, Bukidnon is a historic highland enclave known for its cool climate, towering pine trees, and the world-famous Del Monte pineapple plantations. Originally established in the 1920s as a residential and administrative base for American plantation workers, it has evolved into one of Bukidnon's most beloved tourist destinations.

Visitors are drawn to the iconic scenery of endless pineapple fields, well-preserved colonial-era structures, and the refreshing mountain air that makes Camp Philips a true highland gem. Whether you're here for the scenery, the history, or the famous Del Monte ice cream — Camp Philips delivers an unforgettable experience.

Tour the Pineapple Fields

Walk through one of the world's most iconic agricultural landscapes — the endless Del Monte pineapple fields of Bukidnon.

A Golden Sea of Pineapples

The Del Monte pineapple plantations of Manolo Fortich stretch across the Bukidnon highlands in breathtaking rows as far as the eye can see. These world-renowned fields have been producing premium pineapples since the 1920s and remain one of the most photographed agricultural landscapes in the Philippines.

Walking tours along the plantation roads let visitors get up close to the fields, observe the farming process, and appreciate the sheer scale of Bukidnon's pineapple heritage. Sunrise visits are particularly stunning — the golden light over the green and golden fruit creates an unforgettable panorama.

Must-Try Treat

The Famous Del Monte Ice Cream

No visit to Camp Philips is complete without tasting the legendary Del Monte pineapple ice cream.

A Scoop of Bukidnon Heritage

The Del Monte pineapple ice cream is arguably the most beloved food souvenir of Manolo Fortich. Made from fresh, locally harvested pineapples grown right in the surrounding plantations, this ice cream captures the pure, sweet essence of Bukidnon's premium fruit in every creamy scoop.

Available at small stores and kiosks near the Camp Philips area, the ice cream comes in several variants — original pineapple, mixed tropical fruit, and seasonal specialties. Visitors consistently rave about the natural sweetness and freshness that no commercially packaged product can replicate.

It has become a beloved tradition — locals and tourists alike stop for a scoop (or two!) every time they pass through Manolo Fortich. Many visitors buy multiple cups to take home for friends and family.

Visit Historical Landmarks

Step back in time and explore the rich colonial history preserved within Camp Philips.

🏛️

Del Monte Clubhouse

The historic Del Monte Clubhouse was the social hub for American plantation workers in the early 20th century. The colonial-era structure remains well-preserved and is one of the most photographed heritage buildings in Bukidnon.

Heritage SiteFree Viewing
⛪

Camp Philips Chapel

A charming colonial-era chapel built during the American period, still in use today for religious services. The chapel's simple architecture and serene surroundings of pine trees make it a peaceful and historic landmark.

Religious SiteOpen to Public
🏘️

American-Era Houses

Rows of well-maintained American colonial residential houses line the roads of Camp Philips. These bungalow-style homes with wide verandas and lush gardens reflect the lifestyle of early 20th-century plantation managers.

Before You Go

Travel Guide & Visitor Tips

Plan your Camp Philips visit with these helpful tips and practical information.

🚌

How to Get There

From Cagayan de Oro City, take a bus or van to Manolo Fortich (1.5–2 hrs). From the town center, hire a habal-habal or tricycle to Camp Philips. Private vehicles are the most flexible option.

🌅

Best Time to Visit

Visit in the dry season (November–April) for the best scenery. Early mornings (6–9 AM) offer the most stunning light over the pineapple fields and cooler temperatures.

🧥

Bring a Jacket

Camp Philips sits at 900m+ above sea level. Early mornings and evenings can be quite cool. A light jacket or hoodie is always recommended especially during November–February.

📷

Photography Tips

The pineapple fields are most photogenic during golden hour (sunrise and sunset). Wide-angle shots capture the vastness beautifully. Drone photography offers spectacular aerial views of the fields.

🍦

Don't Miss the Ice Cream

The Del Monte pineapple ice cream sold near the area is a must-try signature treat. Try the original flavor and mixed fruit variants — both are local favourites sold by roadside vendors.

💵

Bring Cash

ATMs are limited in the area. Bring enough cash for food, ice cream, souvenirs, and habal-habal rides. Local vendors and roadside stalls only accept cash payments.
